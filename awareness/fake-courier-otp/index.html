Banking 2026-04-08

"Your parcel is stuck at customs" — the new courier OTP scam

Fraudsters impersonate FedEx / DHL / Indian Post officials. Here's the script and the red flags.

1-min read · 167 words
Share

One of the fastest-growing scams in metro cities right now: a call claiming a parcel addressed to you is stuck at customs because it contains "illegal items" (drugs, fake passport, banned medicines). The scammer pressures you into "clearing" the parcel — which requires you to share an OTP "for verification."

The script

  1. Call from a "FedEx / DHL / Indian Post customs officer"
  2. They name you and your address (often scraped from data leaks)
  3. They mention illegal contents and threaten arrest within hours
  4. They transfer you to a "police officer" who plays the same intimidation role
  5. To "clear your name," you must verify your identity via OTP

The red flags

  • No courier company calls about customs. Customs is a government agency.
  • Police never arrest you over a phone call. They visit in person, with paperwork.
  • OTP is never required for customs clearance. Period.

If you've shared an OTP

Treat it as an active banking fraud. Call your bank's fraud line, then 1930, then file at /get-help.
